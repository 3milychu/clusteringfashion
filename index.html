Model Clusters

Model clusters are composed of objects that were grouped particularly well when training a machine to look at only form-focused, colorblinded versions of the image dataset. See Model Build for specifics on the machine learning task. The machine was given colorblinded, form-focused versions of the images (refer to Figure 1). The goal is to uncover what patterns in form could be found, and to identify cases where using machine learning to disentangle form was effective and ineffective.

Symmetrical Items

x

Symmetrical items are clustered particularly well when focusing on form. The two clusters you see here are Clusters #46 and #29 in their entirety. This is indicative of one primary hypothesis, that form is particularly effective at grouping items that are produced after mechanical production, when patterns have been standardized.

Footwear

x

Footwear is clustered beautifully and reveal the range of colors across types of form when focusing on form. The clusters shown here have been filtered by footwear, which means that the clusters displayed have been reduced to just clusters that contain footwear, showing only footwear items from each cluster. This shows that clustering items through machine learning can be beneficial when it is desirable to reveal the range of color across a collection that is similar in other ways. This is another indication that form is particularly effective at grouping items that are produced after mechanical production.

<= 16th Century

x

<=16th Century items do not seem to cluster very well when focusing on form. The clusters shown here have been filtered by items from the 16th century or older, which means that the clusters displayed have been reduced to just clusters that contain items from the 16th century or older, showing only items from the 16th century or older in each cluster. This limitation may be due to the relatively smaller number of items in the collection from the 16th century or older. This might also be another indication that form is only particularly effective at grouping items that are produced after mechanical production, since those that were produced before were not as standardized in form.

Disentangling Form: Colorblinding Machines to Draw Geometric Patterns in What We Wear

What if we couldn't see color? How would we, for example, classify fashion objects solely on the basis of their spatial composition?

Machine Learning Classification

This project uses k-means, a statistical model to classify objects from the Metropolitan Museum of Art's 7.6k Costume Institute items. The model was built using machine learning, as an unsupervised learning task.

K-means Model

The k-means model was trained on greyscale versions of images with accentuated outlines. For more information on the model, see Model Build

Understanding the Clusters: An Annotated View

The clusters formed by focusing on colorblinded form are effective in cases and not so effective in others.

In the annotated version, clusters are circled and labeled by 1) Cluster ID, 2) observed cluster characteristics and 3) details regarding the composition of clusters i.e. average year, breakdown of items by body part.
